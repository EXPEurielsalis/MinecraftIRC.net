#minecraft rules

1. General Rules:

  • You must be logged in through NickServ to speak on this channel. Use the command "/msg NickServ help identify" for help logging in and "/msg NickServ help register" for information regarding registration.
  • Not reading the rules is no excuse for not following them. Same goes for the topic.
  • All applicable rules and laws apply. The EsperNet rules can be found in the EsperNet Charter.
  • This is a strictly Safe For Work (SFW) channel. All chat and all linked or otherwise posted content is expected to be PG13. Do not post material unsuitable for children.
  • Do not post any malicious material. This includes exploits for IRC, Minecraft, or computers in general.
  • Do not distribute modified client or server software in this channel. Do not download any minecraft software from this channel either, for your safety.
  • Unauthorized talking bots or scripts are not permitted. Bots are permitted as long as they do not send any unsolicited messages.
  • Don't use auto-rejoin, it irritates the management.

2. Rules Regarding Other Users:

  • Do not harass other users. This includes, but is not limited to, sending unwanted messages or spamming/flooding.
  • Be polite to the users around you. It takes two or more people to have an argument, and all are at fault.
  • If you are experiencing issues with a specific user, it is recommended that you ignore them with the ignore command. Use "/help ignore" for more information. (note that this is a client feature and may not be available on all clients)

3.Rules Regarding Channel Operators:

  • Operate the channel fairly and appropriately.
  • Do not ignore users who try to appeal their bans. Among other things, this encourages evasion.
  • Kick and ban reasons are to be clear and concise, so that they may be easily accessible in the channel logs.
  • It is the job of the operators to enforce the channel rules.
  • Operators should be able to justify their decision regarding removal of a user, and their decision may be overturned by a consensus from the other operators.
  • Operators are to follow the rules of general users, their position does not make them exempt.
  • Operators should not remove bans placed by other operators except under extenuating circumstances.

4. Rules Regarding Consequences:

  • Being kicked from the channel serves as a warning. If you are kicked, keep in mind that further disquiet will likely result in a ban.
  • If you are banned, do not evade it. Period. You will be banned for longer, and possibly banned from the network.
  • In general, if you are banned, you have the right to know why.
  • For ban appeals, you can appeal to any op in the channel. However, under most circumstances, it is up to that person whether your ban is justified.
  • The ban duration shall fit the offense.

5. Important Notes:

  • For issues with Minecraft, see #minecrafthelp. For issues with the Minecraft forum, use #minecraftforums. For modding discussion, see #risucraft. For help with Spigot, see #spigot.
  • This is a Minecraft channel. Discussion related to Minecraft is not required, but should take priority over unrelated chatter.
  • These rules serve as guidelines. Management has discretion to do as it sees fit.

Appendix: Grounds for disciplinary actions:

Grounds for Being Kicked:

  • Trolling, spamming, flooding
  • Advertising (including multiplayer servers; use Minecraft Forums instead)
  • Being a talking bot or script
  • Being the owner of a talking bot or script
  • Begging for voice or operator privileges
  • Impersonating Mojang staff and/or #minecraft voiced users/operators

Grounds for a Short Ban (a few minutes to a few days):

  • Repeated offenses that merit a kick
  • Auto-rejoin after a kick
  • Mildly Not Safe For Work (NSFW) content
  • Picking fights or causing arguments

Grounds for a Long Ban (longer than a few days):

  • Repeated offenses that merit a shorter ban
  • Racism, sexism, or discrimination
  • Evading a ban (+b) or quiet (+q)
  • Pornographic or otherwise obscene material
  • Being a spambot

Grounds for a Permanent Ban (does not expire):

  • Repeated offenses that merit a long ban
  • Blatantly offensive speech

Grounds for a Network Ban:

  • EsperNet Charter offenses
